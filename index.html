Andrew Lin

hi! i am andrew lin, a third-year undergraduate student at the university of chicago. i major in sociology and media arts and design, and i am very passionate about games and play. i seek to both examine them as cultural objects, and try to incorporate my own reflections of sociology into my design process.

i have worked on quite a few team projects, mostly during my coursework. i really value coordination and communication in the game design process. i am also eager to apply my academic learnings, especially my study of the sociology of culture, in real-world contexts.

i am also learning to make websites and thus potentially incorporate them into transmedia projects.

please feel free to browse through my portfolio to see some projects i worked on/am working on! they're mostly group projects - please click on their links for more information.

Suspect Pursuit

suspect pursuit is my first attempt in creating a puzzle with physical objects. centered around train tickets, the puzzle also tries to explore the creative use of qr codes.

Café énigme

welcome to café énigme, a puzzle that involves coffee cups, *actual* macarons, study notes, and all the things you need when studying at a café. can you find the hidden message behind everything?

Left Behind

left behind is a room escape game about death and mourning, about how someone's physical possessions serve as powerful mementos of their lifetime after their passing. the project will be on itch.io soon!
